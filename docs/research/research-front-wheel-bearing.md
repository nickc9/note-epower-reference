# Front wheel bearing / front hub bearing assembly — JDM Nissan Note e-POWER HE12

Research date: 2026-07-04. Target car: 2018 Note e-POWER NISMO S, DAA-HE12.

**Trap check performed throughout:** every source below was checked against the
two-NISMO-S trap (petrol NISMO S = DBA-E12改, 1.6L HR16DE, 5MT, 17", rear
DISCS — a different car). No source describing a manual gearbox, 17" wheels,
or rear discs was used for OEM part-number claims. All minkara posts cited
below explicitly self-identify as "日産 ノート e-POWER" (some tagged "E12" by
minkara's own car-model picker, which lumps e-POWER under the E12 family
label even though the chassis code is HE12 — this is a minkara UI quirk, not
evidence the poster owns the petrol car; content of each post was read to
confirm e-POWER/HR12DE+EM57 powertrain language before use).

Amayama direct part pages **403'd on both attempted numbers this session**
(consistent with prior research files' note that Amayama sometimes blocks
WebFetch even though it works in-browser — not independently re-verified via
browser this session). Confidence below rests on cross-corroboration between
independent JP retailers and multiple US Nissan-dealer parts-catalogue
mirrors instead, per the fallback rule in the project brief.

## Headline conclusion

**OEM front hub bearing assembly for HE12 (Nov 2016 on): P/N 40210-5RB0A.**
Confidence: **verified** (2+ independent sources agree on genuine catalogue
fitment; see §A). This appears to be **shared across the whole post-facelift
E12-family front axle**, not an e-POWER-exclusive number — it also shows up
against Kicks (P15) and even Note E13 (next-gen) listings, consistent with
Nissan/Renault-Nissan practice of carrying one front hub bearing across a
platform family regardless of powertrain. **No evidence found of a distinct
NISMO/NISMO S part number** — treat "all grades share 40210-5RB0A" as
probable-by-inference (front brake hardware is already known to be identical
across HE12 grades per `research-brakes.md`), not independently confirmed for
the bearing specifically.

**The "commonly fails around 100,000 km" premise is only partly
substantiated.** Real owner reports exist of front hub bearing noise/wear on
this car — that part is genuine and well-documented. But the failures found
this session are **scattered from ~36,000 km to ~140,000 km**, including one
case of a bearing (dealer-replaced under a stated "countermeasure/revised
part number", 対策品) failing again after only another **9,642 km**. "100,000
km" is the generic *consumable-item guideline* quoted by owners and mechanic
blogs for wheel bearings on cars in general (not HE12-specific), not a
statistic describing where HE12 failures actually cluster. Report this
honestly on the site: real failure mode, wide/unpredictable mileage range,
not a tight 100k-km pattern.

### A2. Superseded/earlier number — 40210-1HA1A (pre-facelift E12/NE12, Sept 2012–Oct 2016) — NOT for HE12

- **P/N: 40210-1HA1A**, aftermarket cross-ref also WBH-222N (same physical
  bearing/kit branding, different Nissan catalogue number).
- Source: https://yabumoto1.jp/shopdetail/000000182545/ — fetched. Title:
  "ノート E12 NE12 ハブベアリング フロント...WBH-222N H24.09〜H28.10"
  (Sept 2012–Oct 2016, i.e. strictly **before** the HE12/e-POWER launch).
- Also found: https://www.amazon.co.jp/...40210-1HA1A/dp/B076HMJ1CW,
  listed for "ノート E12 NE12 マーチ K13 NK13 HB33_" (search snippet only,
  not independently fetched).
- **This is the pre-facelift petrol-only number and must NOT be published as
  an HE12/e-POWER part number.** It's included here only to document the
  supersession point: the facelift that introduced HE12 (Nov 2016) is also
  where the front hub bearing catalogue number changed from 1HA1A to 5RB0A.
  This timing (bearing number changes exactly at the facelift, not at any
  powertrain split within the facelift-era lineup) is itself evidence that
  the bearing is a body/chassis-generation part, not an e-POWER-specific one.
- **Confidence: verified** (2 independent sources, explicit non-HE12 date
  range) that this number is wrong for the owner's car — useful as a
  do-not-use flag more than as a positive finding.

### A3. NISMO / NISMO S — no distinct number found

- No source (JP retailer, Amayama attempt, NISMO official catalogue, or
  forum) gives a NISMO-specific front hub bearing part number.
- Indirect supporting reasoning only: `research-suspension-nismo.md` (§A)
  established via an official Nissan spec sheet that front brake type
  (ventilated disc) and wheel spec (16×6.5J, 195/55R16) are **identical**
  between e-POWER NISMO and e-POWER NISMO S, and `research-brakes.md`
  established front brake hardware (rotor/caliper/pad) is shared across ALL
  HE12 grades including NISMO/NISMO S with no distinct NISMO SKU found. Since
  hub bearing bore/flange is normally dictated by the knuckle/hub casting and
  disc-brake interface rather than wheel diameter, it's reasonable to expect
  40210-5RB0A also covers NISMO/NISMO S, but **this is inference, not a
  confirmed fitment table hit** — flag as probable-by-analogy only, not
  verified, if publishing a NISMO-inclusive applicability claim.
- One forum data point is mildly corroborating: a mechanic's blog
  (https://ameblo.jp/met4cdf8/entry-12914522740.html, fetched) describes a
  front hub bearing replacement as "preventive maintenance" on a car
  explicitly noted as **NISMO-spec** — i.e., NISMO cars do get ordinary hub
  bearing service, not some categorically different part/procedure. Doesn't
  give a part number.

### A4. ABS wheel speed sensor integration — likely integrated, not independently confirmed for this exact P/N

- Could not find a source explicitly stating "40210-5RB0A includes the ABS
  tone ring/sensor" or, conversely, a separate discrete ABS sensor part
  number for the HE12 front hub (searched for a "47945-xxxxx"-pattern
  number specifically; nothing usable surfaced).
- General industry/mechanic-blog corroboration that this is the norm on
  modern Nissans (not Note-specific, treat as generic background only):
  - https://www.team-mho.com/hub-b-change-cost/ (fetched): "ベアリング内蔵
    タイプになってます。この部品が高い" — modern hub bearings integrate the
    ABS sensor/tone ring, which is why they cost more than older separate
    bearing+sensor designs.
  - https://seibishinote.com/hub-bearing/ (fetched, discussing a Honda Fit,
    NOT the Note — included only as generic mechanic-knowledge background):
    confirms same integrated-sensor pattern industry-wide and that fitting
    orientation relative to the ABS sensor is a reason DIY is discouraged.
- **Verdict: unconfirmed for this specific P/N, but plausible/likely given
  it's standard practice on comparable modern Nissan models** (front discs +
  ABS on all grades per §A above). Do not publish as a stated fact without
  a source naming the sensor explicitly; word it as "likely integrated,
  unconfirmed" if included in guide text.

**Assessment:**
- The failure mode (roaring/humming noise that increases with speed,
  sometimes with detectable play) is real, well-documented, and specific
  enough (multiple independent owners, consistent symptom description,
  consistent repair pattern) to be worth a guide entry.
- The **mileage spread is wide (36k–140k+ km) and skews younger than "100k km
  common failure point" would suggest** — several reports are well under
  100,000 km, including one under-50k-km case with a documented recurrence
  after a factory "countermeasure" part was fitted, which if anything reads
  as evidence of an early-life durability/design issue in some units rather
  than a predictable wear-out point at high mileage.
- Both front AND rear hub bearings appear in owner reports; this is not
  exclusively a front-wheel phenomenon. The task brief asked specifically
  about front bearings — worth noting on the site that rear bearing failure
  is also reported, in case a future research pass wants to cover the rear
  part number too (not researched here — rear uses a drum-integrated
  pressed bearing per one shop blog, likely a different Nissan P/N pattern,
  e.g. commonly "43200-series" on Nissan FWD cars, not verified this
  session).
- No single source ties a specific ~100,000 km figure to a genuine
  statistical claim (recall count, TSB, dealer service-bulletin data, or
  even a large sample of owner reports) — every "100,000 km" mention found
  is a generic wear-item rule of thumb repeated by owners/mechanics, applied
  to wheel bearings on cars generally, not a Note/HE12-specific finding.
- **Recommended site framing:** document this as "front (and rear) hub
  bearing noise is a real, repeatedly-reported issue on HE12 e-POWER cars,
  occurring anywhere from ~35,000 km to 140,000+ km — inspect if you hear a
  speed-dependent roaring/humming noise, don't wait for a specific mileage."
  Do not state "commonly fails around 100,000 km" as if that were the
  median/typical failure point — the evidence doesn't support that precision.

### Trap check on evidence sources
- The NISMO-specific data point (ameblo blog) explicitly states NISMO trim
  and describes e-POWER-consistent maintenance context (not 5MT, no mention
  of 17" wheels or rear discs) — passes trap check, this is the e-POWER
  NISMO, not the petrol NISMO S.
- One additional minkara post found in search
  (https://minkara.carview.co.jp/userid/2288020/car/2719682/7631159/note.aspx,
  fetched) is tagged "NISMO S (E12)" and describes front hub bearing
  replacement that did **not** fix a periodic low-frequency noise — the
  underlying cause was eventually traced to the transmission/reduction gear,
  not the bearing. Read carefully: the post text uses e-POWER-consistent
  language (owner speculates "heavy front-end weight of e-POWER" stresses
  the drivetrain) and describes a "5-year warranty" boundary, with no
  mention of a manual gearbox or 17" wheels — **judged to be the e-POWER
  NISMO S, not the petrol trap car**, but flagging that minkara's own model
  tag ("NISMO S (E12)") does not disambiguate the two cars, so this judgment
  rests on internal post content only, not the site's own categorization.
  Useful mainly as a caution: front hub bearing noise and drivetrain/gearbox
  noise can be confused with each other on this car, and replacing the
  bearing doesn't always fix a "roaring" complaint.

## C. DIY feasibility and cost

- **Not a simple DIY job for most owners.** Multiple independent sources
  agree the front hub bearing is a press-fit assembly requiring knuckle
  removal:
  - Generic mechanic-blog corroboration (not Note-specific, background
    only): https://www.team-mho.com/hub-b-change-cost/ and
    https://seibishinote.com/hub-bearing/ (both fetched) — "ナックルを取り外
    してプレスにて交換をする作業になります" (remove the knuckle, exchange
    using a hydraulic press); both explicitly recommend professional service
    over DIY due to the press/special-tool requirement and ABS-sensor
    orientation sensitivity.
  - However, at least one Note e-POWER owner did do it as a home DIY job
    with a press: https://minkara.carview.co.jp/userid/106239/car/3216633/7386653/note.aspx
    (fetched) quotes aftermarket bearing ¥4,719 vs. OEM ¥7,579 (parts-only,
    per side), plus new nut/cover fasteners (non-reusable) — so it's
    DIY-feasible **if the owner has access to a hydraulic press or engine
    crane + suitable adapters/puller**, not a simple hand-tool job.
  - A DIY YouTube video specifically for Note E12 front hub bearing
    removal/installation exists (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fx3bR-LhhOE,
    surfaced in search, not independently fetched/transcribed this session)
    — flagging as a lead, not verified content.
- **Shop labor cost observed:** ¥41,800 (ex-tax) for three bearings in one
  visit (https://minkara.carview.co.jp/userid/3459709/...) — call it
  roughly ¥12,000–15,000 labor per corner at that shop, plus parts. Generic
  (non-Note-specific) mechanic-blog figures for a comparable FWD car (Honda
  Fit) quote front bearing all-in (parts+labor) around ¥18,800 per side
  (https://seibishinote.com/hub-bearing/) — in the same ballpark, offered
  only as a sanity-check range, not a Note-specific quote.
- **Non-reusable fasteners:** owner reports consistently note the hub
  lock-nut and hub cap are one-time-use and must be replaced (matches the
  general Nissan pattern already documented for driveshaft nuts in
  `research-suspension-nismo.md` §D, 228 N·m one-time-use driveshaft nut —
  the hub lock-nut is a related but distinct fastener at the wheel-bearing
  interface, torque figure not independently found this session for the
  HE12 specifically; one non-Note mechanic blog quotes a generic 200 N·m
  hub lock-nut torque, not verified for this car).
- **Verdict: press/specialist-tool job, "advanced DIY at best" — recommend
  the site frame this as shop work for most owners**, same tier as strut/
  knuckle work rather than a routine consumable swap like brake pads.

## Addendum — browser-verified Amayama check (2026-07-04, same-day follow-up)

The open question in item 2 below was chased down with live browser access
(Claude in Chrome), which the project's Amayama workaround says should work
even when WebFetch 403s. Result: **it contradicts this file's headline
conclusion above.**

- `https://www.amayama.com/en/part/nissan/402105rb0a` (browser-verified):
  the "may fit to" fitment table lists **ONLY Nissan Kicks, engine HR16DE**.
  No Note fitment is shown at all, despite the JP retailer and US-dealer
  corroboration in §A1 above explicitly naming "Note HE12."
- `https://www.amayama.com/en/part/nissan/402101ha1a` (browser-verified):
  the fitment table explicitly lists **Nissan Note** among its models, with
  engines **HR12DDR, HR12DE, HR12DR, HR15DE, K9K, K9K.63KW** — HR12DE is the
  e-POWER generator engine code. No date cutoff or "superseded by" banner
  appears on the page, even though §A2 above cites JP retailers stating this
  number is pre-facelift-only (Sept 2012-Oct 2016, before HE12 existed).

So the two evidence types disagree in opposite directions: JP retailer
date-range labeling says 5RB0A = HE12, 1HA1A = pre-facelift-only; Amayama's
own genuine fitment lookup shows the reverse (Note appears under 1HA1A's
fitment table, not 5RB0A's). Given the project's own precedent elsewhere
(e.g. `rear-brake-drum.yaml`, where a genuine Amayama fitment hit was
preferred over a retailer-only "Note E12" label with zero Amayama fitment
data), a genuine catalogue hit normally outweighs retailer labeling — but
here the retailer evidence is unusually specific (exact H28.11〜 / H24.09〜
H28.10 date ranges matching the HE12 launch date precisely), so this reads
as a genuine unresolved conflict rather than a case where one source is
obviously wrong.

**This has been published to the site as `confidence: unconfirmed`** (per
the project's own definition: "unconfirmed = forum-only/conflicting") rather
than picking a winner — see `src/content/parts/front-wheel-bearing.yaml`.
Do not upgrade this to verified/probable without either (a) a dealer parts
lookup against a real HE12 frame number, or (b) an Amayama diagram-view
fitment breakdown by generation (the diagram link on both part pages did not
open without further interaction this session — CAPTCHA or login wall
suspected, consistent with the project's documented Amayama catalogue-browser
gate).

The failure-evidence table in §B and the DIY/cost assessment in §C are
unaffected by this addendum and were published as-is (mileage spread,
symptom description, and press-job difficulty don't depend on which OEM
number is correct).
