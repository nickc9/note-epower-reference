## What this is (and isn't)

The e-POWER has **no transmission and no CVT**. The EM57 motor drives the
front wheels through a fixed single-speed reduction gear, which holds
about **1.94 L of Nissan Matic Fluid S**. If a garage quotes you for "CVT
fluid" (NS-2/NS-3), they're looking at the petrol Note — wrong car, wrong
fluid.

## The interval question

Nissan publishes **no change interval** — the reduction gear is treated as
sealed-for-life. Owner teardown logs tell a more nuanced story. Across
four independent logs, drained fluid at 18,000-54,000 km consistently
came out discoloured with visible iron particles (one owner at ~40,000 km:
"iron powder clumps about 1 mm"; another at ~54,000 km: "covered in iron
powder"). Several report the drivetrain feeling/sounding smoother after a
change. None reported problems from *not* changing it.

Honest framing: this is **optional preventive maintenance**, not a
required service. A single-speed gearbox on a smooth electric motor is a
gentle environment, and the metal in early fills is largely break-in
wear. If you're keeping the car long-term, a change somewhere around
50,000 km is cheap insurance (dealer cost reported around ¥6,000-9,000
all-in, ~30 min job); if not, Nissan's position is that you can leave it
alone.

## The job

Two 10 mm hex plugs on the reduction gear housing: a drain plug
underneath (magnetic — inspect what it's caught) and a fill plug (access
varies by log — some worked top-down from the engine bay, some from
below). Drain, refit the drain plug with a **new crush washer** (torque
~34 N·m per one owner log — forum-grade figure), pump in Matic Fluid S to
the fill hole, refit the fill plug with a new washer.

e-POWER safety habit applies as always: car fully **OFF**, not READY —
the engine can start itself in READY, and you're working next to it.
