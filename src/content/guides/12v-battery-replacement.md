## Overview

The e-POWER's 12V battery is **under the boot floor** (not the engine
bay), and it's a **European EN/DIN "LN" format — not a JIS size** like
petrol Notes. NISMO, NISMO S, X, and Medalist take **LN2 (60 Ah class)**;
only the stripped base "e-POWER S" grade takes the smaller LN1. Ignore
retail listings that say "Nismo S = LN1" — they're confusing the grade
names; still, check the label on your installed battery before ordering.

Buy an **EFB / idling-stop-rated** battery (GS Yuasa "-IS" suffix, Bosch
Black EFB, Panasonic Caos LN2): the e-POWER cycles its 12V battery harder
than a conventional car, and standard flooded types die early.

The 12V battery only runs the computers, locks, and lights — the car
starts the engine from the traction battery. But a dead 12V battery still
means the car won't go READY.

## ⚠️ The vent hose

The battery sits in the enclosed boot, so it has a **hydrogen gas vent
hose** connected to a nipple on the battery. **Reconnecting it is
safety-critical** — venting hydrogen into a closed boot is a fire/explosion
risk. Make sure your replacement battery has the vent port on the correct
side (LN-format batteries have ports both ends; use the same end as the
old one).

## Steps

1. Power the car fully off, remove the key from the car, open the boot,
   and lift out the boot floor panel.
2. (Recommended) Connect a memory backup device so you keep the clock,
   trip computer, and power-window auto function. Without one, nothing is
   damaged — you just re-teach settings afterwards.
3. Disconnect the **negative (−) terminal first**, then the positive (+).
4. Disconnect the vent hose from the battery nipple, undo the hold-down
   clamp (10mm), and lift the battery out — an LN2 weighs ~16-17 kg, mind
   your back over the boot lip.
5. Fit the new battery, clamp it down, and **reconnect the vent hose**.
6. Reconnect the **positive (+) first**, then the negative (−).
7. If you didn't use a memory backup, re-teach the driver's window
   anti-pinch: power to READY (or ACC), run the window fully open, then
   fully closed **holding the switch up for a few extra seconds** at the
   top. Repeat for other auto windows if fitted. Reset the clock.

## Notes

- **No dealer/CONSULT registration is required** after replacement — none
  of the sourced owner replacement logs needed any scan-tool step.
- The genuine Nissan part number for this battery is unconfirmed (one
  forum post reports an Atlas-branded battery stamped 24410 0542R). The
  aftermarket LN2 fitments in the parts entry are the reliable route.
- Old battery disposal: any battery retailer or scrapyard takes them, and
  in Japan most online sellers include a collection ticket
  (回収伝票) option.
