## Overview

The engine air filter (**16546-5WK0A** / Pitwork **AY120-NS067**) sits in
the airbox on top of the engine, left side of the bay as you face the car.
Nissan doesn't publish a fixed JDM replacement interval for the element —
it's a "replace when dirty" inspection item at the 12-month service.
e-POWER duty is gentle on it (the engine runs less than a conventional
car), but city dust doesn't care; every 2 years is a sensible default.

## Steps

1. Power the car fully off (not READY) and open the bonnet. The engine can
   otherwise start itself at any time — treat the bay as live until the
   car is off.
2. Locate the black plastic airbox and release the spring clips around the
   lid rim.
3. Lift the lid enough to slide the old element out. Note which way the
   element faces before removing it.
4. Wipe loose debris out of the box with a damp cloth — don't drop
   anything into the intake opening.
5. Seat the new element the same way round (rubber sealing lip up against
   the lid), close the lid, and re-clip.

## Notes

- If the lid won't seat flush, the element isn't seated — don't force the
  clips.
- No panel filter "performance" upgrade is needed or useful here; the
  engine is capped by its generator role.
