## Overview

Factory cell is a **CR2025** (per Nissan's official FAQ). A CR2032 is
slightly thicker but fits and lasts longer — a common owner substitution.
When the fob battery dies entirely you can still start the car: hold the
fob against the power button (the car reads it by proximity) and unlock
the door with the mechanical blade key hidden in the fob.

## Steps

1. Slide the release catch on the back of the fob and pull out the
   mechanical blade key.
2. Insert a cloth-wrapped flat-blade screwdriver into the seam the blade
   key vacated and twist gently to split the two case halves.
3. Note the cell orientation — **positive (+) side faces down** into the
   base half — and swap in the new cell. Don't touch the circuit board or
   its contacts (skin oils corrode them).
4. Snap the halves together, reinsert the blade key, and test lock,
   unlock, boot release, and that the car goes to READY.

## Notes

- Nissan officially recommends dealer replacement to avoid case damage —
  in practice the job is trivial if you pry at the seam, not the corners.
- All HE12 grades use the same cell; higher grades (Medalist, NISMO,
  NISMO S) just have more proximity-sensing door handles, not a different
  key battery.
