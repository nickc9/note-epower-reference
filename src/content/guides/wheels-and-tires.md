## Sizes by grade

| Grade | Tire | Wheel | Offset | PCD |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| S / X / Medalist / Autech | 185/65R15 88S | 15 × 5.5J | ET40 | 4×100 |
| NISMO / NISMO S | 195/55R16 87V | 16 × 6.5J | ET46 | 4×100 |

Hub bore 60 mm (single-source — probable). OE tires: Bridgestone B250
(standard), **Yokohama DNA S.Drive** (NISMO/NISMO S — identical on both).

**Don't trust generic databases for "Note NISMO S"**: many list the older
petrol E12 NISMO S (17-inch, 205/45R17) — a different car. See the
[model identification guide](../identify-your-model/).

## Pressures

Door-placard value: **230 kPa front / 210 kPa rear** (two independent
owner sources quoting the placard). Caveat: not confirmed from a
NISMO-specific placard photo — check your own door jamb sticker; if it
differs, trust the sticker (and tell us via GitHub).

## No spare wheel

The HE12 e-POWER carries a **puncture repair kit** (sealant + compressor)
instead of a spare. Check the sealant bottle's expiry date — they're
typically 4-6 years and most imports are past it.

## ⚠️ Jacking points

- The four **sill pinch-weld points** are for the factory scissor jack,
  one wheel at a time.
- Front floor-jack point: the round reinforced dimple centered behind the
  front bumper area.
- **Rear caution:** two independent owner sources warn that the obvious
  U-shaped channel in the middle of the rear underbody **bends** — the
  torsion beam area lacks reinforcement. Use the **rear tow hook** as the
  floor-jack point instead. (Owner-forum consensus, not factory-manual
  verified — flagged accordingly.)
- No e-POWER-specific battery/underbody jacking hazard was found in any
  source, but this is absence of evidence; the traction battery lives
  under the front seats, so stay on designated points.

## Wheel removal / rotation

1. On flat ground, chock the wheels staying on the ground, crack each nut
   half a turn before lifting.
2. Lift at a proper jacking point and support on a stand — never work
   under a car on the jack alone.
3. Rotate front-to-rear on the same side (directional tires) or use a
   cross pattern (non-directional). The DNA S.Drive is asymmetric but not
   directional — cross rotation is fine; keep the OUTSIDE sidewall marking
   out.
4. Refit: snug the nuts in a star pattern, lower the car, then torque to
   **108 N·m** in a star pattern.
5. Re-check torque after ~100 km, especially on alloy wheels.
