## Overview

All HE12 grades — including NISMO and NISMO S — share the same front
caliper, pad, and 260 mm vented rotor. Every aftermarket catalogue lists
one part number "incl. NISMO/NISMO S", and NISMO's own S-tune upgrade pad
fits all grades. (Whether the factory NISMO *compound* differs inside the
same part is unresolved — it doesn't change what you order.)

**The e-POWER wrinkle:** regenerative braking does most of the day-to-day
slowing, so friction brakes on these cars often wear slowly but *rust and
seize* instead — especially imports that sat at docks. Sticky slide pins
and corroded rotor edges are more likely findings than worn-out pads.
Inspect yearly even if mileage is low.

## Wear limits

- Pads: plan replacement at **3 mm**, replace immediately at **2 mm**
  (generic JIS-adjacent guidance — no Note-specific factory figure found;
  new pads are ~10 mm)
- Rotors: minimum thickness **20 mm** (from 22 mm new), measured with a
  micrometer away from the rust lip

## Steps

1. Power the car fully off. Chock the rear wheels, crack the front wheel
   nuts, jack and support the front on stands (see the wheels & tires
   guide for jacking points), remove the wheels.
2. Inspect through the caliper window: pad thickness both sides, rotor
   face condition, even wear. Uneven inner/outer wear = sticking slide
   pins.
3. Remove the lower slide pin bolt, swing the caliper up (or remove both
   and hang the caliper on wire — never let it hang by the hose).
4. Note the anti-rattle shim arrangement, then remove the old pads.
5. Clean the carrier's pad abutment clips with a wire brush, grease the
   contact points and slide pins with rubber-safe brake grease.
6. Check the reservoir first (level rises as you press), then press the
   piston back with a piston tool or C-clamp against the old pad.
7. Fit new pads (transfer/renew shims), swing the caliper back, and
   tighten the slide pin bolt firmly — no HE12-specific factory torque
   figure was found in research; typical Nissan small-car slide pins run
   ~30-38 N·m. Snug with a short wrench if in doubt.
8. Refit wheels (108 N·m), then **pump the brake pedal until firm before
   moving** — the first press after piston push-back does nothing.
9. Bed the pads: 10–15 moderate stops from ~50 km/h, avoiding full stops
   and hard braking for the first ~200 km. On an e-POWER, use B-range /
   low regen or brake firmly enough to engage the friction brakes —
   gentle braking may be pure regen and won't bed pads at all.

## Notes

- Brake work is safety-critical: if any of this is unfamiliar, have a
  professional do it and use this page to check parts and prices instead.
- Rear brakes are drums — see the brake system reference guide.
