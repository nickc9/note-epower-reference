## The trap

"Note NISMO S" names **two different cars**, and English-language sources
constantly mix them up:

| | Petrol NISMO S | e-POWER NISMO S |
|---|---|---|
| Chassis code | DBA-E12改 | **DAA-HE12** |
| Years | ~2014-2017 | Sept 2018 on |
| Powertrain | 1.6L HR16DE, 5MT | 1.2L HR12DE generator + EM57 motor |
| Wheels | 17-inch | **16-inch** |
| Rear brakes | Disc | **Drum** |

If a listing, review, or parts database describes a manual gearbox,
17-inch wheels, or rear discs — it's the old petrol car. Order nothing
from that page.

**Check your chassis code** on the VIN plate (door jamb / engine bay):
e-POWER cars are **HE12** (DAA-HE12), 4WD e-POWER is SNE12/SNE13, petrol
is E12/NE12.

## e-POWER NISMO vs e-POWER NISMO S

Mechanically they are nearly the same car (all verified from JP launch
coverage and Nissan sources):

**Shared:** body reinforcement set (6 points), NISMO-tuned suspension,
16×6.5J ET46 wheels with 195/55R16 Yokohama DNA S.Drive, front vented
discs + rear drums, aero kit.

**NISMO S adds:**
- Motor/inverter tune: **100 kW / 320 N·m** (vs 80 kW / 254 N·m) — done in
  the inverter, VCM, and battery computer, plus a strengthened reduction
  gear
- **B range** with its own Normal/S/ECO modes (6 drive patterns vs 3),
  tuned one-pedal behaviour
- LED headlights standard (optional on NISMO)
- Optional NISMO-tuned **Recaro** front seats

**Practical consequence for parts:** almost every service part on this
site — filters, oil, battery, wipers, tires, brakes — is identical between
e-POWER NISMO and NISMO S, and mostly identical to other HE12 grades. The
differences that matter are in the tune, not the hardware you'll be
replacing.

## Quick self-check

- Badge says NISMO S + push-button start + no gear lever gate for manual =
  e-POWER NISMO S ✔
- Your car is a 2018 import with e-POWER badging = HE12. Everything on
  this site applies.
