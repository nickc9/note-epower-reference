## Door mirrors

The mirror housing itself (motor, fold mechanism, glass, integrated turn
signal, blind-spot camera bracket) is genuine Nissan **96301-5VA0C (RH) /
96302-5VA0C (LH)** — confirmed directly against Amayama's genuine
catalogue, engine fitment HR12DDR/HR12DE (the e-POWER generator code).
Nissan has many 96301-series siblings differing by paint color and
equipment level, so this is the number for a camera-equipped,
turn-signal-integrated mirror; match your own car's spec before ordering.

NISMO separately sells a **dry-carbon mirror cover** (9637S-RNE20) that
bolts over the factory housing — a left+right pair, ¥77,000 tax-in.
NISMO's own current grade-applicability table confirms this is available
across **all four** NISMO-badged grades: NISMO, NISMO S, e-POWER NISMO,
and e-POWER NISMO S. It's shared with the unrelated Leaf (ZE1) and
Fairlady Z (RZ34), so don't be surprised seeing it cross-listed for those.

## Mudguards

Genuine JDM mudguards use the **F38E0-xxxxx** family, color-coded to the
car's paint — **F38E0-6XJ1C** is confirmed by a real owner purchase/fit
on a Note e-POWER in Aurora Flare Blue Pearl (¥18,565 at a Nissan dealer).
Match the suffix to your own paint code; a different color will need a
different number. That owner's car was 4WD/AUTECH-spec and could only
fit the **front** set — whether 2WD grades can run front+rear together
isn't confirmed. Don't confuse this with the US-market 999J2-xxxxx
mudguard family, which is a different regional part number scheme for
different vehicles entirely.

## Floor mats

NISMO's official accessory catalogue lists four generation-specific SKUs:

| Part number | Fitment |
|---|---|
| 74900-RNE20 | All grades, pre-facelift (through Nov 2016) |
| 74900-RNE21 | Post-facelift, gasoline |
| **74900-RNE22** | **Post-facelift, e-POWER** (excl. cold-climate spec, excl. 4WD) |
| 74900-RNE23 | Post-facelift, e-POWER cold-climate spec (excl. 4WD) |

All are 5-mat sets with the current NISMO logo and red stitching, all
shown production-discontinued in the catalogue at time of writing. This
is a dealer-orderable **accessory**, not exclusive to the NISMO S — NISMO's
own grade-applicability table shows it available across all four grades.
No source states whether NISMO S ships with it as standard equipment.

## Recaro seats (NISMO/NISMO S option)

The e-POWER NISMO and NISMO S offer an optional NISMO-tuned RECARO
semi-bucket sport seat, front seats only, co-developed with RECARO (not
just RECARO-badged) — confirmed by three independent Japanese auto-media
sources. Reported prices vary (¥275,000 to ¥396,000 across sources,
not reconciled) — confirm current pricing with a dealer.

**Material is not confirmed.** Despite it being repeated online, no
directly-sourced article states the seat is leather+Alcantara — treat
that claim as unverified until a Nissan spec sheet or brochure confirms
it. If you do have Alcantara-type suede sections, generic (non-Note-
specific) best practice is: damp-cloth wipe for routine care; for deeper
cleaning use a fabric-safe cleaner made for suede/Alcantara, applied to a
microfiber cloth (not sprayed directly onto the seat), blot rather than
scrub; avoid solvents, degreasers, silicone/oil-based products, wax, and
steam cleaners; no protectant products; spot-test first. No genuine
reupholstery kit or dedicated seat cover product was found for this seat.

## Horn

Nissan's general convention (well-attested across many models, not
Note-specific) is 25610-xxxxx = high tone, 25620-xxxxx = low tone. A
specific Note/HE12 number (25620-5WK0A) turns up repeatedly in retailer
listings, but a direct Amayama check in Phase 4 came back **"Product not
found"** — so this project is not publishing it. No corresponding
high-tone number was found at all. Genuine horn numbers for this car
remain unconfirmed; if you have a dealer invoice, please contribute it.

## Engine / motor / reduction-gear mounts — the biggest remaining gap

No genuine OEM mount number was confirmed for the e-POWER powertrain
(HR12DE generator-engine mount, drive-motor mount, reduction-gear mount,
or torque rod). Amayama's JDM catalogue — the source most likely to have
this — returned 403 on every attempt, and a "11220-5WK0A" number that
recurred in web search summaries **does not exist** on Amayama (checked
directly, July 2026) — treat that number as a search-synthesis artifact,
not a real part.

One thing IS now settled: NISMO's own **Reinforced Engine Mounts**
product (三点セット: 11210/11220/11360-RSK30) is real, but it's for the
**petrol** NISMO S only (HR16DE) — NISMO's current grade-applicability
table shows it available for NISMO S but explicitly **not** for e-POWER
NISMO or e-POWER NISMO S. If you were hoping to upgrade your e-POWER's
mounts with a NISMO part, NISMO doesn't currently sell one — this is a
genuine gap, not just an unresearched one. Stock e-POWER mount numbers
still need a dealer parts-counter lookup or an accessible JDM parts
diagram to resolve.

Pulled directly from NISMO's own current parts-catalogue matrix
(nismo.co.jp, browser-verified July 2026) — useful for knowing what
NISMO does and doesn't offer for the e-POWER grades specifically. A
representative sample (○ = available, − = not available):

| Engine Oil Cooler Kit | − | ○ | − | − |

The pattern is consistent: drivetrain-specific items (mounts, flywheel,
LSD, oil cooler, muffler, strut tower bar) are petrol-manual-only —
they depend on hardware (clutch, conventional exhaust routing, a strut
brace point) the e-POWER either doesn't have or doesn't share. Cosmetic
and universal-fit items (mirror cover, floor mat, brake pads, wheel
nuts, tower bar) are available across all four grades.
