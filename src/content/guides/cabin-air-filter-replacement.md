## Overview

The cabin filter (**27277-1HD0A**, Pitwork **AY684/685/686-NS018** by
grade) lives in the blower housing behind the glovebox — the standard
E12-family location. Three Pitwork grades exist: basic pollen (AY684),
pollen+odor (AY685), and premium PM2.5 (AY686) — all the same physical
size.

## Steps

1. Open the glovebox and empty it.
2. Release the glovebox from its stops: squeeze the sides inward (and
   unhook the small damper arm on the right side if fitted) so the box
   swings fully down.
3. Behind it you'll see the filter cover on the blower housing — a
   rectangular lid with tabs. Squeeze the tabs and pull the cover off.
4. Slide the old filter out. Expect leaves and grit — have a bin bag
   ready.
5. Slide the new filter in with the **airflow arrow (↓ / AIR FLOW) in the
   same orientation as the old one** (down, into the blower). Check the
   old one's arrow before you bin it.
6. Refit the cover and clip the glovebox back onto its stops.

## Notes

- If the new filter seems too big, it's folded — the element should slide
  in without bending the pleats.
- A/C smells that survive a filter change usually mean the evaporator
  needs a foam cleaner treatment — a separate job.
