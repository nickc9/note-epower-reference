## The interval question, settled

You'll see two numbers floating around. Nissan's generic maintenance page
says plugs every 20,000 km — that applies to **conventional** plugs.
Nissan's own FAQ states that **platinum/iridium-family plugs are a
100,000 km scheduled item**, and the HE12's factory plug (22401-ED815,
NGK precious-metal equivalent) is exactly that type. Owner evidence backs
it up: a documented owner pulled the original plugs at exactly 100,000 km
as planned maintenance and found them worn but still serviceable.

**So: 100,000 km.** With e-POWER engine-hours being lower than the
odometer suggests, plugs are a non-event on these cars for a long time.

## Parts

Three plugs. Genuine **22401-ED815**; NGK OE-equivalent **LZKAR6AP-11**;
or the **NGK Premium RX LKAR6ARX-11P** that JDM owners overwhelmingly fit
at replacement (~¥2,500/set). Precious-metal plugs come pre-gapped —
don't re-gap them, and never gap-check with force on the fine electrode.

## Steps

1. Power the car fully off and let the engine cool — the e-POWER engine
   may have been running recently without you noticing, and plugs in an
   aluminium head must be removed cool.
2. Remove the engine cover. The three coil-on-plug units sit along the
   top of the engine.
3. Unclip each coil connector, remove the 10 mm coil bolt, and pull the
   coil straight up. Work one cylinder at a time if you want to be safe
   on connector routing.
4. Blow or brush debris away from the plug wells before unscrewing —
   anything next to the plug falls into the cylinder when it's out.
5. Remove each plug with the 14 mm thin-wall socket. Inspect: even
   light-brown/grey wear is normal; oil fouling or heavy soot on one
   cylinder is worth investigating.
6. Thread new plugs in **by hand** until seated (cross-threading an
   aluminium head is the expensive mistake here), then torque to
   ~20 N·m — or NGK's fallback for a new gasketed plug: 1/2 turn past
   hand-tight. One owner found the factory plugs under-torqued after
   years of heat cycles, so a torque wrench is worth using.
7. Refit coils (snug the 10 mm bolts), connectors, and the cover. Power
   to READY and force the engine to run (CHARGE mode) to confirm smooth
   running.

## Notes

- Same plugs and interval for all grades — the NISMO S tune lives in the
  inverter, not the engine internals.
- If a plug fights you on the way out, stop, add penetrating oil, and
  work it back and forth — a seized plug is a garage job, not a battle.
